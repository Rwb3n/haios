# Causal Thinking

Trace chains of cause and effect. Identify root causes rather than symptoms. Map how changes propagate through a system.

## When to use
- Something broke and you need to find why
- Understanding side effects of a change
- Tracing a chain of events to its origin

## Guiding questions
1. What is the observed effect?
2. What are the possible causes? Which is most likely and why?
3. Is this the root cause, or a symptom of something deeper?
4. What other effects does this cause produce?
5. If we fix this cause, does the effect reliably go away?

## Output structure
- Observed effect
- Causal chain (A caused B caused C)
- Root cause identification
- Confidence level and reasoning
