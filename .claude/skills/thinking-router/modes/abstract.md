# Abstract Thinking

Extract general principles from specific instances. Move between concrete details and higher-level patterns. Identify the essence of a problem separate from its context.

## When to use
- Designing something that needs to work across multiple contexts
- Spotting patterns across seemingly unrelated problems
- Deciding what's essential vs incidental in a design

## Guiding questions
1. What is the concrete problem or instance?
2. What general principle or pattern does this represent?
3. Where else does this pattern appear?
4. What would a solution to the general case look like?
5. Does the general solution still fit the specific case?

## Output structure
- Concrete instance
- Abstracted principle
- Other instances of the pattern
- General vs specific solution comparison
