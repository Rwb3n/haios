# Dialectical Thinking

Explore opposing viewpoints to find synthesis. Present thesis and antithesis, then work toward a resolution that incorporates the strongest elements of both.

## When to use
- Two valid but opposing approaches exist
- Stakeholders disagree on direction
- A decision has clear trade-offs on both sides

## Guiding questions
1. What is the thesis — the strongest argument for one side?
2. What is the antithesis — the strongest argument for the other?
3. Where do they genuinely conflict vs where is it only apparent conflict?
4. What synthesis incorporates the best of both?
5. What does each side get wrong?

## Output structure
- Thesis (strongest form)
- Antithesis (strongest form)
- Points of genuine vs apparent conflict
- Synthesis
