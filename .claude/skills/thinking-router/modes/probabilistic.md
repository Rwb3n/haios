# Probabilistic Thinking

Reason about likelihoods and distributions rather than certainties. Consider the range of possible outcomes and their relative probabilities.

## When to use
- Outcomes are uncertain and multiple scenarios are plausible
- Need to reason about risk and expected value
- Binary thinking (will/won't happen) is too simplistic

## Guiding questions
1. What are the possible outcomes?
2. What is the rough probability of each?
3. What is the expected value across outcomes?
4. What are the tail risks — unlikely but high-impact scenarios?
5. How sensitive is the conclusion to changes in probability estimates?

## Output structure
- Outcome space
- Probability estimates with reasoning
- Expected value analysis
- Tail risks
- Sensitivity to assumptions
