# Nonlinear Thinking

Explore feedback loops, emergent behavior, and systems where effects are not proportional to causes. Recognize when small inputs create large outputs and vice versa.

## When to use
- Systems with feedback loops or cascading effects
- When a small change produces unexpectedly large consequences
- Analyzing complex systems with interacting components

## Guiding questions
1. What are the feedback loops in this system?
2. Are any of them reinforcing (amplifying) or balancing (dampening)?
3. Where might small inputs create disproportionate outputs?
4. What emergent behaviors arise from component interactions?
5. Are there tipping points or thresholds?

## Output structure
- System components and their interactions
- Feedback loops identified (reinforcing/balancing)
- Nonlinear effects and thresholds
- Emergent behaviors
