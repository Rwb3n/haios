# Counterfactual Thinking

Examine "what would have happened if" alternatives. Analyze past decisions or events by considering different paths. Useful for learning from outcomes and evaluating decision quality.

## When to use
- A decision led to an unexpected outcome — was it the decision or luck?
- Evaluating whether a past choice was correct given what was known
- Learning from failures or successes

## Guiding questions
1. What actually happened? What was the outcome?
2. What decision or event is the pivot point?
3. If that had gone differently, what would the outcome likely have been?
4. Was the original decision reasonable given available information at the time?
5. What should be learned for future similar decisions?

## Output structure
- Actual outcome
- Pivot point (decision/event)
- Counterfactual outcome
- Decision quality assessment (separate from outcome)
- Lessons for future
