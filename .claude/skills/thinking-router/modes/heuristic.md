# Heuristic Thinking

Apply practical rules of thumb and mental shortcuts. Use experience-based strategies to find good-enough solutions quickly. Recognize when precision isn't worth the cost.

## When to use
- Need a quick, practical decision without exhaustive analysis
- The problem space is too large for systematic evaluation
- Experience or established patterns can guide the decision

## Guiding questions
1. What rules of thumb apply here?
2. Have I seen a similar problem before? What worked?
3. What's the 80/20 — which small effort yields most of the result?
4. Where would precision cost more than the value it adds?
5. What are the known failure modes of these heuristics?

## Output structure
- Applicable heuristics
- Historical precedent
- 80/20 analysis
- Heuristic risks and when to abandon them
