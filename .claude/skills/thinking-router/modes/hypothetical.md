# Hypothetical Thinking

Construct and test "what if" scenarios. Explore possibilities by imagining conditions that don't yet exist. Use thought experiments to stress-test ideas.

## When to use
- Exploring future scenarios or potential changes
- Stress-testing a design or plan
- Need to reason about situations you haven't observed

## Guiding questions
1. What scenario am I constructing? What changes from reality?
2. Given that change, what follows logically?
3. What breaks under this scenario?
4. What new opportunities or risks appear?
5. How plausible is this scenario actually occurring?

## Output structure
- Scenario description (what changes)
- Logical consequences
- What breaks / what works
- Plausibility assessment
