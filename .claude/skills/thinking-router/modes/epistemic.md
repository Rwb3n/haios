# Epistemic Thinking

Examine the boundaries of knowledge. Distinguish between what you know, what you don't know, and what you can't know. Assess the reliability of your information sources.

## When to use
- Making decisions with incomplete information
- Evaluating whether you have enough information to proceed
- Questioning assumptions that might be wrong

## Guiding questions
1. What do I know with high confidence? What is the evidence?
2. What do I not know that would change my approach if I did?
3. What assumptions am I making? Which are testable?
4. How reliable are my information sources?
5. What can't be known at this stage, and how should I proceed despite that?

## Output structure
- Known (with confidence levels)
- Unknown (with impact if discovered)
- Assumptions (testable vs untestable)
- Information gaps and how to proceed
