# Bayesian Thinking

Update beliefs based on evidence. Start with priors, incorporate new data, arrive at posteriors. Make the strength of your beliefs proportional to the evidence.

## When to use
- Evaluating competing hypotheses
- Incorporating new evidence into existing beliefs
- Estimating likelihood when direct data is scarce

## Guiding questions
1. What is my prior belief? How strong is it and why?
2. What new evidence am I observing?
3. How likely is this evidence under my hypothesis vs alternatives?
4. How should I update my belief given this evidence?
5. What evidence would make me change my mind significantly?

## Output structure
- Prior belief with reasoning
- Evidence observed
- Likelihood ratio (how much more likely under hypothesis vs alternatives)
- Updated belief (posterior)
- What would change the conclusion
